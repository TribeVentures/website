Overview

Overview

A business model is not a business plan. Traditional business plans are written based on research and projected assumptions. These assumptions are not market-tested and therefore, are not convincing.

Purpose

To equip W&M students with advanced entrepreneurial endeavors by identifying problems, initiating potential solutions, validating the solutions by testing the market and pivoting for better ones.

Why

A business model, under the popular Business Model Canvas format, shows visually the logic of how a company intends to make money after testing customers, suppliers, distributors, etc.

Requirements

Step-by-step requirements
  1. Form a team. Teams must be comprised of 2 – 5 W&M students. A leader must be appointed for each team. Bonus points will be given to teams with diverse education backgrounds such as business and non-business majors, seniority of students (graduates, upperclassmen, and underclassmen), etc.
  2. Attend the workshop on March 17th: Teams are highly recommended to participate in the workshop.
  3. Register: Team leaders must register team participation by filling out this form.
  4. Take advantage of the following resources: Channel “Rollins Center for Entrepreneurship & Technology at BYU”. Others: Business Model Generation, Rough Draft VC’s starter kit
  5. Submit the deliverables: By the end of April 02nd, team leaders must email the following to bmc.wm1693@gmail.com. 1) A Link to Multimedia presentation. The format should be a video presentation up to 10 minutes long and must be uploaded on Youtube. The presentation should tell the story of how the business model was formed, evolved and pivoted through customers’ feedback. The business model canvas will be the primary tools for communicating the works. 2) PDF file: Content: Slides used in the video.
  6. Attend the Final Event: All teams are highly recommended to attend. 6 best teams will be selected to pitch in front of judges and audience. The pitch content can be similar to Multimedia presentation or with information updates A total cash prize of $1,500 will be awarded to the winning teams

Judging Criteria

Criteria
  1. If the students use the business model canvas
  2. If the students clearly state their assumptions/hypothesis: identify the most crucial assumptions to test first (the one that would kill their model)
  3. If students designed low cost, rapid, but reliable tests of these hypotheses
  4. If students conducted the tests in a reliable manner: at least 5 customers must be interviewed and what students have learned from the interviews
  5. If the students pivoted the models after customers’ feedback and if a pivot is supported by evidence or if they failed to pivot when the evidence clearly stated it
  6. If students have developed a prototype or minimum viable product (MVP) and how this MVPs have been improved
